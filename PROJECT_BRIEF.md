# Angel Doctor Website Project Brief

## 1. Project Name

Angel Doctor

## 2. Project Type

GEO-first international medical travel and healthcare coordination website.

## 3. Core Positioning

Angel Doctor helps international patients access trusted healthcare in China through AI-assisted hospital matching, transparent coordination, trained local medical escorts, and structured offline patient support.

Angel Doctor is not a hospital, clinic, doctor group, or medical diagnosis provider. Angel Doctor is a healthcare coordination and medical travel support platform for international patients seeking care in China.

## 4. Core Brand Message

### Hero Value Proposition

Efficient Care. Fair Price. Clear Guidance.

### Brand Mindset Slogan

China Healthcare, Guided End to End.

### Core Website Description

Angel Doctor helps international patients access trusted healthcare in China through AI hospital matching, transparent coordination, and trained local medical escorts.

### User-Facing Benefit Line

Access China’s top hospitals with a dedicated medical escort by your side.

## 5. What Angel Doctor Is

Angel Doctor is an international patient support platform built around China’s healthcare access, hospital coordination, and local medical escort system.

Angel Doctor helps patients:

Understand suitable healthcare options in China.

Match with appropriate hospitals, departments, clinics, or healthcare providers.

Coordinate appointments and visit preparation.

Navigate Chinese hospitals and clinics.

Receive local support through trained medical escorts.

Understand pricing components before committing.

Continue communication and follow-up after the visit when available.

Angel Doctor provides coordination, translation support, appointment assistance, medical travel support, and local medical escort services. Medical diagnosis, treatment plans, prescriptions, procedures, and clinical decisions are made by licensed healthcare providers.

## 6. What Angel Doctor Is Not

Angel Doctor must not be positioned as:

A hospital.

A clinic.

A doctor group.

A treatment marketplace.

A simple hospital directory.

A low-cost medical tourism agency.

A company that guarantees appointments.

A company that guarantees treatment outcomes.

A substitute for licensed medical advice.

A company that directly diagnoses, prescribes, or treats patients.

## 7. Strategic Opportunity

International patients often face poor choices when seeking healthcare:

Their home country has long waiting times.

Private healthcare is too expensive.

Dental care is too costly.

They want access to Chinese hospitals but do not know how to enter the system.

They cannot understand the Chinese medical system.

They have Chinese family background but do not live in China.

They need a second medical opinion.

They want complex treatment but do not have a trusted coordinator.

They arrive in China with no one to accompany, translate, coordinate, or guide them through the hospital.

This is Angel Doctor’s major opportunity: to make Chinese healthcare access understandable, coordinated, and supported for international patients.

## 8. Target Users

Angel Doctor serves international patients who are considering medical care in China.

Primary user groups include:

### 8.1 Overseas Patients Seeking Faster Care

Patients who face long wait times in their home country and want faster access to healthcare options in China.

### 8.2 Cost-Sensitive Private Healthcare Users

Patients who find private healthcare in their home country too expensive and want to compare care options in China.

### 8.3 Dental Patients

Patients seeking dental implants, crowns, veneers, dental checkups, or dental treatment planning in China.

### 8.4 Health Checkup Users

Patients seeking executive health checkups, preventive screening, imaging, blood tests, and specialist review.

### 8.5 Second Opinion Patients

Patients who want another medical opinion from suitable hospitals or specialists in China.

### 8.6 TCM and Recovery Users

Patients seeking TCM recovery, acupuncture, pain relief, sleep support, fatigue recovery, stress recovery, or wellness-focused programs.

### 8.7 Overseas Chinese Families

Patients who have Chinese family background but do not live in China, cannot easily navigate hospitals, or do not want to rely only on relatives for medical coordination.

### 8.8 Short-Stay Travelers to China

Travelers who are visiting China and want dental care, health checkups, TCM recovery, medical escort support, or light medical coordination during their stay.

### 8.9 Patients Needing Local Hospital Support

Patients who already have or plan to have a hospital or clinic visit in China and need help with registration, navigation, translation, payment guidance, examination routing, pharmacy, report collection, or follow-up coordination.

## 9. Core User Pain Points

International patients often struggle with:

Not knowing which hospital or city is suitable.

Not knowing how to book or coordinate a hospital visit.

Not understanding Chinese hospital departments and workflows.

Not knowing what medical records to prepare.

Not knowing whether a public hospital, private clinic, dental provider, TCM provider, or health checkup center is suitable.

Not understanding pricing components.

Not speaking Chinese.

Not knowing how to register, pay, collect reports, or navigate large hospitals.

Not knowing how to communicate with doctors or hospital staff.

Not having someone reliable with them during the visit.

Not knowing how to handle follow-up after the visit.

Not trusting random agents or informal interpreters.

Angel Doctor’s strongest differentiation is not only hospital access. It is the combination of:

China hospital and healthcare provider coordination.

Standardized local medical escort services.

GEO-first educational content that helps users understand their choices.

## 11. 600+ Medical Escorts: Positioning Requirement

The website must make clear that Angel Doctor’s 600+ medical escorts are not casual freelancers or temporary helpers.

They should be positioned as a trained, managed, and standardized offline service network.

The website should communicate that Angel Doctor has:

A medical escort training system.

Service standards.

Backend coordination.

Privacy agreements.

Complaint handling.

Emergency response mechanisms.

Service records.

Clear task scope.

Coordinator oversight.

Follow-up communication support.

The goal is to turn “medical escort” into a standardized product, not make it look like Angel Doctor is temporarily finding someone to accompany the patient.

## 12. Medical Escort Product Definition

Angel Doctor’s medical escort service helps patients navigate hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers in China.

A trained local medical escort may support:

Hospital or clinic arrival.

Registration assistance.

Department navigation.

Payment guidance.

Communication support.

Translation support where available.

Examination routing.

Pharmacy guidance.

Report collection.

Follow-up coordination.

Local visit logistics.

Patient handoff to Angel Doctor coordinators.

Medical escorts do not provide medical diagnosis, treatment decisions, prescriptions, or clinical recommendations.

## 13. Offline Service System to Communicate

The website should make the offline system visible and credible.

Core modules to communicate:

### 13.1 Training System

Angel Doctor medical escorts follow a training process covering hospital workflow, patient communication, privacy rules, basic service standards, visit documentation, and escalation procedures.

### 13.2 Service Standards

Medical escort service should be presented as a structured service with defined support tasks, clear boundaries, and documented expectations.

### 13.3 Backend Coordination

Angel Doctor coordinates patient needs, city selection, appointment preparation, escort assignment, hospital visit support, and follow-up communication through a backend coordination process.

### 13.4 Privacy Agreements

Patient information and medical inquiry details should be handled under privacy agreements and clear data protection rules.

### 13.5 Complaint Handling

The service system should include a way for patients to raise issues, request follow-up, or report service problems.

### 13.6 Emergency Mechanism

Angel Doctor should communicate that urgent issues during service can be escalated internally. Angel Doctor is not an emergency medical service provider. For medical emergencies, patients should contact local emergency services immediately.

### 13.7 Service Records

Escort visits should be supported by service records, task tracking, and coordinator follow-up where available.

## 14. Business Model

Angel Doctor’s business model is built on medical coordination, local patient support, and packaged services.

Angel Doctor has established a China-based offline medical escort system and escort management framework. The company generates revenue through:

Productized service packages.

Medical escort service fees.

Care coordination service fees.

Medical travel support services.

Optional add-on services such as translation support, medical record preparation, follow-up coordination, and local visit support.

Hospital medical fees are paid directly by the patient to the hospital, clinic, or healthcare provider.

Angel Doctor should not position itself as collecting or controlling hospital medical fees unless this is legally and operationally confirmed.

## 15. Pricing Positioning

Pricing should be transparent but cautious.

The website should explain cost components:

Hospital or provider medical fees.

Angel Doctor coordination fees.

Medical escort fees.

Translation or medical record preparation fees if applicable.

Travel, hotel, and local transportation costs.

The website should not promise:

Guaranteed lowest price.

Exact price before consultation.

No hidden costs guaranteed.

Guaranteed appointment.

Guaranteed doctor access.

Guaranteed outcome.

Use safer wording:

Estimated costs depend on provider confirmation, diagnosis, treatment plan, materials, doctor availability, medical record review, city preference, timeline, and follow-up needs.

## 32. Schema Requirements

Use JSON-LD only.

Schema must match visible content.

Do not add fake schema values.

Do not mark Angel Doctor as a Hospital or Physician unless legally accurate.

Do not use fake reviews, fake ratings, or unsupported aggregateRating schema.

Do not add exact prices in schema unless verified, published, and maintained.

Recommended schema by page type:

Homepage: Organization, WebSite, WebPage, FAQPage if FAQ exists.

Primary content flow:

Reddit / Instagram / Search / GEO content→ Educational guide or destination page→ Treatment or hospital access explanation→ Trust-building page→ Get Your Care Plan or Book Local Medical Escort→ Coordinator follow-up→ Service package sale

The website should be designed for users who arrive with questions, not users who are already ready to buy.

Therefore:

Use clear educational content.

Give quick answers.

Explain the process.

Make the first form lightweight.

Do not force users to upload medical records.

Do not push “Buy Now” as the default CTA.

Build trust before asking for commitment.
